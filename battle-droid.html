Project Overview

Developed a fully detailed Battle Droid Assembly in SolidWorks using reference images and mechanical assembly design. I created custom modeled geometry and joint systems using spline tools, advanced extrusions, and dimension refinement to replicate and customize the original B-1 Battle Droid design.

By utilizing trial and error I turned reference imagery into a realized physical model that emphasizes realism (especially the head), assembly integration, and detailed component interaction. The head alone uses over 15 distinct SolidWorks modeling features.

Individual Components

Each part was modeled independently before being mated into the final assembly.

Head

Almost geometrically exact to the actual B-1 design. Uses over 15 SolidWorks modeling features.

Upper Torso

Primary structural body piece — connects the head, arms, and lower torso.

Lower Torso

Hip connection component linking the upper body to the leg assemblies with pivot mate.

Upper Arm

Shoulder-to-elbow segment with ball joint geometry for rotational articulation.

Lower Arm

Elbow-to-hand segment — includes fine detail at the claw-tip for accuracy.

Upper Leg

Hip-to-knee linkage designed for articulated standing and walking poses.

Lower Leg & Foot

Knee-to-foot segment with the characteristic swept-back ankle geometry of the B-1 design.
