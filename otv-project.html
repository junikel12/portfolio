Project Overview

The goal of this project was to design a fully autonomous Over Terrain Vehicle (OTV) capable of completing tasks at a mission site while navigating through an obstacle course. I was responsible for the drivetrain and bracket design systems of the OTV.

To select motors and wheels, I completed drivetrain calculations focused on torque, speed, and budget constraints. I also utilized SolidWorks, Fusion360, and Onshape to design and 3D print custom brackets for motors, wheel axles, sensors, and movement arms integrated throughout the vehicle.

Propulsion System

The propulsion system consists of the drive motor, wheel, and inner and outer wheel caps. Motor selection was driven by torque and speed calculations to meet terrain and mission requirements.

The inner wheel stabilizer was designed to connect the wheel interface with the motor axle by transforming a circular aperture into a D-shaft profile — allowing the wheel to transmit torque without slipping.

Custom Bracket Design

Each bracket was designed from scratch in SolidWorks, toleranced to specific component specs, and 3D printed for rapid iteration and deployment.

Motor Bracket

Holds the drive motor firmly against the main chassis. Toleranced to the motor's exact specs so the motor is squeezed into position — preventing drift and limiting variance in path straightness and turn distance (in rad).

Linear Actuator Bracket

Designed to hold a linear actuator rigidly against the chassis to prevent movement during operation, ensuring precise and repeatable actuation.

Color Sensor Bracket

Mounts the color sensor underneath the main chassis. Features a snap-fit design that lets the sensor click into place and stay secure throughout all movement phases.
