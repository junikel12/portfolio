Project Overview

The goal of this project was to design a truss with the best weight-to-force ratio possible. My team researched various truss configurations and analyzed how they reacted to a point force applied at the center before selecting a Howe truss design.

I custom made all of the gusset plates in SolidWorks using intricate geometric calculations to ensure they occupied the full 15% of each member allotted for bonding area. I then laser cut the plates for integration into the truss, and assisted in calculating the force each member experienced and whether members were in tension or compression.

Testing & Failure Analysis

The truss was loaded with a central point force and monitored to failure. It failed at 515 lbs due to gusset plate shear — first at the bottom joint (highest stress concentration from multiple members converging), then at the top.

Top gusset plate shear. A construction gap between members reduced effective bonding area below the design target, creating a direct path to failure under load.
Bottom gusset plate shear. The bottom joint concentrated forces from multiple tension and compression members simultaneously — making it the first to fail under natural stress.
Force (lbf) vs. displacement (in) recorded by the testing press. The truss reached a maximum load of 515 lbs before gusset plate shear initiated catastrophic failure.
